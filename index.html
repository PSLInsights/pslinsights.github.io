Multan Sultan

Multan Sultans, a powerhouse in Pakistani T20 cricket, proudly represents Multan in the dynamic Pakistan Super League (PSL). Established in 2017, the team secured its place as the sixth entrant with a notable investment of US$41.6 million spread over eight seasons, translating to an impressive US$5.2 million per season. Amidst the thrilling cricketing action, Multan Sultans found their home ground at the iconic Multan Cricket Stadium. Peshawar Zalmi

Peshawar Zalmi, a dynamic Twenty20 cricket team in Pakistan, proudly represents the energetic city of Peshawar in the thrilling Pakistan Super League (PSL). Founded in 2015 alongside the launch of the inaugural PSL by the Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB), the team boasts Javed Afridi as its enthusiastic owner. Under the captaincy of the skilled Babar Azam and the coaching guidance of Daren Sammy, Peshawar Zalmi has evolved into a formidable force in the PSL. Kamran Akmal holds the distinction of being the leading run-scorer for the team, while Wahab Riaz showcases his mastery as the leading wicket-taker. The franchise's genesis in 2015 was part of the PCB's unveiling of owners for city-based franchises. Javed Afridi's acquisition of the Peshawar franchise underscored his commitment to the team's success. This investment set the stage for Peshawar Zalmi's exciting journey in the dynamic realm of the Pakistan Super League.

Lahore Qalandar

Lahore Qalandars, a prominent Pakistani cricket franchise in the Pakistan Super League (PSL), proudly represents Lahore, the capital of Punjab. Owned by the Rana Brothers, the team's home ground is the iconic Gaddafi Stadium. Currently led by Shaheen Afridi and coached by Aaqib Javed, a former Pakistani cricketer, Lahore Qalandars has made significant strides in recent seasons. Historically, the team faced challenges, finishing at the bottom of the points table in the initial four PSL seasons. However, a turnaround occurred in the 2020 edition when they reached the final and clinched the championship. The success continued in the 2022 PSL edition, securing another title. Fakhar Zaman stands as the leading run-scorer, and Shaheen Afridi holds the title of the leading wicket-taker for the team. In the franchise's inception in 2015, the PCB announced the sale of the Lahore franchise to Kausar Rana Resources Private Limited Company for US$25.1 million over a decade, making it one of the most expensive teams in PSL history. With the introduction of the Sixth Team in 2018, Lahore Qalandars became the third most expensive franchise in the league. In 2016, the franchise initiated the Players Development Program (PDP), conducting "Jazz Rising Stars" trials across eight Pakistani cities to identify emerging talent. Notably, Yasir Jan, an ambidextrous fast bowler from Rawalpindi, was discovered during this program by former Pakistan fast bowler Aqib Javed.

Islamabad United

Islamabad United, a leading Pakistani T20 franchise in the Pakistan Super League (PSL), proudly represents the capital, Islamabad. Established in 2015, the team is owned by Leonine Global Investments, led by Amna Naqvi and Ali Naqvi through their sports entity Leonine Global Sports. With two PSL titles, Islamabad United is the most successful franchise in league history. Their home ground is the Rawalpindi Cricket Stadium, and they are currently coached by Mike Hesson, with Shadab Khan as the youngest captain in PSL history. Luke Ronchi and Shadab Khan lead the team as the top run-scorer and wicket-taker, respectively. In 2015, the Islamabad franchise was acquired by Leonine Global Sports for US$15 million, laying the foundation for their remarkable journey in the Pakistan Super League.

Quetta Gladiators

Quetta Gladiators, a key player in Pakistani Twenty20 cricket in the Pakistan Super League (PSL), competes with fervor in the league. Established in 2015 as a result of the formation of the PSL by the Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB), the Gladiators claimed the championship in PSL 2019.Nominally based in Quetta, the provincial capital of Balochistan, Pakistan, the team plays most home games at the Gaddafi Stadium. The Bugti Stadium serves as their home ground. The captaincy is held by Sarfaraz Ahmed, with Shane Watson as the head coach, Azam Khan as the team's manager, and Abdul Razzaq as the assistant coach. Sarfaraz Ahmed stands as the leading run-scorer for the team, while Mohammad Nawaz holds the title of the leading wicket-taker. In December 2015, the PCB unveiled the owners of the Quetta franchise, with Omar Associates, a Karachi-based company, acquiring the team for US$11 million, marking the beginning of their journey in the Pakistan Super League.

Karachi Kings

Karachi Kings, a prominent Pakistani Twenty20 cricket franchise in the Pakistan Super League (PSL), fiercely competes in the league. Established in 2015 by the Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB), the team is based in Karachi, the provincial capital of Sindh. Their home ground is the iconic National Stadium. Under the coaching guidance of Phil Simmons and the leadership of Shan Masood, Karachi Kings clinched their first PSL title in PSL V by defeating Lahore Qalandars in the final on 17 November 2020. Babar Azam stands as the leading run-scorer for the side, while Mohammad Amir holds the title of the leading wicket-taker. In 2015, the PCB announced the owners of the initial five city-based franchises, with the Karachi franchise sold to the ARY Media Group for an impressive US$26 million over a ten-year period. Although, in 2017, Multan Sultans replaced Karachi Kings as the most expensive team in PSL history after being sold for US$41.6 million for an eight-year agreement.
